What Makes a Good Reference Video
Green Flags (Steal These Immediately)
	•	✅ One Clear Idea
	•	✅ Clear Face in First Frame
	•	✅ Natural "Lived-In" Lighting
	•	✅ Visible Product Interaction
	•	✅ Conversational Delivery
	•	✅ Simple, Relatable Background
	•	✅ Direct Eye Contact
	•	✅ iPhone Quality
Red Flags (Run Away From These)
	•	❌ Professional Production
	•	❌ No Face Visibility
	•	❌ Overly Scripted
	•	❌ Complex Scenes or Fast Cuts
	•	❌ Poor Product Visibility
Simple, direct, conversational UGC with one clear idea is the easiest to replicate and has the highest success rate.

Building Your Character Blueprint
You've got the perfect first frame. Now we feed it to Gemini to extract a technical description. This description is what you will paste into your prompts later to ensure your AI actor looks the same across every damn video.
The Process:
	•	Open Gemini (or your preferred AI image analysis tool).
	•	Upload your clean first-frame screenshot.
	•	Use the following prompt framework. Copy and paste it.
The Character Consistency Prompt:
Analyze the person in this image and generate a reusable character description based on the following template. Be extremely detailed and technical. Include:
- Exact measurements between facial features (mm between eyes, eye width, nose length, etc.)
- Eye shape, color, and characteristics
- Nose structure and proportions
- Lip shape and fullness
- Face shape and jawline structure
- Cheekbone placement and prominence
- Skin tone and texture details
- Hair color, texture, and style
- Any distinctive features or characteristics
Be extremely detailed and technical. I need to be able to recreate this face from your description alone. And fill this template below:

Identifying Patterns in Winners
After you've analyzed your top 10-20 references, look for the patterns that print money.
Strategic Patterns:
	•	Dominant Lead Type: Is your niche dominated by Problem-Solution hooks ("Tired of sleepless nights?") or Promise-driven hooks ("The 7-second trick to deep sleep")?
	•	The Big Idea Angle: Are the winning ads focused on the speed of the result, the ease of use, or the unique ingredient?
Execution Patterns:
	•	Setting: Do most winners use bedrooms? Is natural lighting dominant?
	•	Delivery: What's the average energy level? How fast do they talk?
	•	Structure: When do they show the product? How long until the call to action?
Example Pattern You Might Find:
"Most winning supplement UGC uses a Problem-Solution lead. They start with a relatable complaint about sleep. The setting is almost always a bedroom with warm, golden hour lighting. The character holds the product at chest level throughout, speaking in a calm, conversational tone. The hook happens in the first 2 seconds, and they show the label clearly at the 3-second mark while emphasizing the key benefit with a head nod."
This pattern becomes your default template for your first batch of 50 tests.
