Understanding Desires:
Mass Desire = "The public spread of a private want"
When millions of people privately want the same thing, it becomes a mass desire that advertising can harness.
Key Rule: You cannot CREATE desires. You can only CHANNEL existing desires onto your product.
________________


How Desires Are Born: The Two Forces
Permanent Forces (Always There)
* Mass Instincts: Universal human drives (health, attractiveness, security)
* Technological Problems: Ongoing issues (car repairs, slow internet)
* These create stable, recurring desires that never go away
Forces of Change (Create New Desires)
* Style Trends: What becomes fashionable (swimming pools vs. bigger cars)
* Mass Education: What society learns (health awareness, new technologies)
* Cultural Shifts: Changes in values (women's liberation, environmental consciousness)
* These create emerging desires and shift existing ones
________________


The Three Desire Categories
Once a desire exists (from the forces above), it falls into one of these types:
Physical Desires
* Health, strength, attractiveness, freedom from pain, sensory satisfaction
Material Desires
* Money, possessions, status symbols, security, convenience
Emotional/Social Desires
* Love, acceptance, recognition, success, power, adventure

________________


How to Find & Evaluate Desires
Step 1: Spot the Desire
   * Watch for complaint patterns
   * Study successful competitors
   * Follow cultural trends
   * Look where people spend money
Step 2: Test Its Power (The 3 Dimensions)
Urgency: How desperately do they want relief? Staying Power: Does this desire renew continuously? Scope: How many people share this desire?
Step 3: Choose the Strongest
Pick the desire with the highest combination of all three dimensions.
________________


The Process in Action
1. Forces Create Desire Example: Social media culture (Force of Change) → People want to look "Instagram-worthy" (New Mass Desire)
2. Categorize the Desire
"Instagram-worthy" = Physical desire (appearance) + Emotional desire (social recognition)
3. Channel onto Your Product Beauty brand: "Get that perfect selfie glow with our new foundation"
________________


Schwartz's Key Insights
Never Fight Desires: The Edsel failed because it fought the mass desire for compact cars
Ride the Wave: Successful companies spot desires early and channel them onto their products
Timing is Everything: Same product can succeed or fail based on which desires are rising/falling
Specificity Wins: Don't just say "people want to be attractive" - find the specific way they want to be attractive right now
________________


Your Job as a Marketer
   1. Be a Desire Detective: Constantly watch for emerging desires and shifting patterns
   2. Pick Your Lane: Choose one powerful desire to focus on
   3. Channel, Don't Create: Direct existing desires onto your product
   4. Stay Alert: Desires change - be ready to pivot when they do
The Bottom Line: Master this, and you can predict what people will want to buy before your competitors even know there's a market for it.
