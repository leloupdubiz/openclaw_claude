## 5. GREAT LEADS - Michael Masterson

### 📖 INFORMATIONS
- **Auteur :** Michael Masterson (Mark Ford)
- **Année :** 2006
- **Pages :** ~250
- **Priorité :** 🟠 HIGH
- **Concept :** "The six easiest ways to start any sales message"

### 🎯 CONCEPT CENTRAL
> "The lead is the most important part of any sales message. It determines whether the reader continues or stops."

Le lead (accroche/début) est la partie la plus critique de tout message de vente. Il détermine si le lecteur continue ou abandonne après les premières secondes.

### 📚 CONTENU DÉTAILLÉ

#### CHAPITRE 1 : THE IMPORTANCE OF THE LEAD

**Pourquoi le lead est crucial :**
- 80% des lecteurs ne dépassent pas le lead
- C'est votre seule chance de capturer l'attention
- Il détermine le ton et la promesse de tout le message
- "The first 100 words are more important than the next 10,000"

**Les 3 Jobs du Lead :**
1. **Grab attention** (Capturer l'attention)
2. **Create interest** (Créer l'intérêt)
3. **Push to continue reading** (Pousser à continuer)

#### CHAPITRE 2 : THE SIX TYPES OF LEADS

**TYPE 1 : THE OFFER LEAD**
- Commence directement par l'offre
- Fonctionne pour audiences chaudes (know/like/trust établi)
- Structure : "Get [produit] for [prix]"
- **Exemple :** "For a limited time, you can get my complete Copywriting Mastery Program for just $497..."

**Quand l'utiliser :**
- Liste chaude (email list engagée)
- Produit déjà connu
- Audience Most Aware

**TYPE 2 : THE PROMISE LEAD**
- Promesse de résultat/bénéfice
- Le plus courant et efficace
- Structure : "How to [bénéfice] in [temps/conditions]"
- **Exemple :** "How to write copy that sells like crazy – even if you've never written a word of advertising in your life..."

**Variantes :**
- "Discover how to..."
- "Learn the secrets of..."
- "Revealed: The method for..."

**Quand l'utiliser :**
- Produit nouveau
- Audience Problem/Solution Aware
- Besoin d'éduquer sur le résultat possible

**TYPE 3 : THE PROBLEM-SOLUTION LEAD**
- Aggrave le problème d'abord
- Puis présente la solution
- Structure : Problem → Agitation → Solution teaser
- **Exemple :** "Are you tired of writing copy that falls flat? Of ads that get ignored? Of sales letters that don't sell? There's a reason... and it's not what you think..."

**Éléments clés :**
- Identifier le problème précis
- Aggraver la douleur
- Montrer que le prospect n'est pas seul
- Teaser de solution (pas complète)

**Quand l'utiliser :**
- Problem Aware audience
- Pain point fort et reconnu
- Besoin d'empathie

**TYPE 4 : THE STORY LEAD**
- Raconte une histoire personnelle
- Créer connexion émotionnelle
- Structure : Setup → Conflict → Resolution teaser
- **Exemple :** "Three years ago, I was broke. My business was failing. I couldn't sell a thing. Then one day, a copywriter friend showed me a simple formula that changed everything..."

**Types d'histoires :**
- Personal transformation
- Discovery story
- Customer success story
- Rags to riches
- Overcoming obstacles

**Quand l'utiliser :**
- Besoin de connexion émotionnelle forte
- Building credibility through experience
- Complex product nécessitant contexte

**TYPE 5 : THE SECRET LEAD**
- Promet révéler un secret
- Créer curiosité intense
- Structure : "The secret to [désir] is..."
- **Exemple :** "The secret to writing million-dollar copy isn't talent. It's not creativity. And it's definitely not 'years of practice.' It's something much simpler... and I'm about to reveal it to you..."

**Variantes :**
- "What [experts] don't want you to know..."
- "The truth about [sujet]..."
- "What nobody tells you about..."

**Quand l'utiliser :**
- Marché saturé (besoin de differentiation)
- Information cachée ou contre-intuitive
- Audience sceptique

**TYPE 6 : THE NEWS LEAD**
- Présente l'information comme nouvelle
- Time-sensitive
- Structure : "Breaking news..." / "New discovery..."
- **Exemple :** "New research from Harvard Medical School reveals a shocking truth about weight loss that contradicts everything you've been told..."

**Éléments :**
- Nouveauté réelle ou perçue
- Source crédible
- Implication directe pour reader

**Quand l'utiliser :**
- Actualité produit/découverte
- Trend riding
- Lancement

#### CHAPITRE 3 : THE BUCKET BRIGADE

**Définition :**
Phrases de transition qui maintiennent le momentum et poussent le lecteur à continuer.

**Pourquoi ça marche :**
- Créer tension/curiosité
- Faire une promesse implicite
- Break la monotonie

**Exemples de Bucket Brigades :**
- "But here's the thing..."
- "And then something strange happened..."
- "You might be wondering..."
- "It gets better..."
- "But wait, there's more..."
- "Here's why..."
- "What's the catch?"
- "The answer may surprise you..."
- "Let me explain..."
- "Here's the kicker..."

**Utilisation :**
- Tous les 2-3 paragraphes
- En début de section
- Après une promesse
- Avant une révélation

#### CHAPITRE 4 : THE 4-LEG STOOL

Un lead efficace doit avoir 4 pattes solides :

**1. URGENCY**
- Pourquoi maintenant ?
- Deadline, scarcity, trend
- "Before it's too late..."

**2. USEFULNESS**
- Bénéfice clair et spécifique
- "How to..." ou "Discover..."
- Promesse tangible

**3. UNIQUE**
- Différenciation
- "Unlike anything you've seen..."
- Angle frais

**4. ULTRA-SPECIFIC**
- Chiffres précis
- Détails concrets
- "In 30 days" > "Soon"

#### CHAPITRE 5 : LEAD LENGTH

**Court vs Long :**

**Short Lead (100-300 mots) :**
- Produit simple
- Audience chaude
- Offre claire
- Exemple : "Get X for Y"

**Long Lead (500-1000+ mots) :**
- Produit complexe/nouveau
- Besoin d'éducation
- Story-driven
- Exemple : Story lead détaillée

**Règle :** As long as it needs to be, but no longer.

#### CHAPITRE 6 : TESTING LEADS

**Comment tester :**
1. Écrire 3-5 leads différents pour même produit
2. Split test avec même body copy
3. Mesurer : Open rate, read rate, conversion
4. Winner becomes control
5. Test nouvelle variation contre control

**Éléments à tester :**
- Type de lead (Story vs Promise vs Problem)
- Longueur
- Ton (urgent vs calme)
- Hook spécifique

### 💡 PRINCIPES CLÉS MASTERSON

1. **"The lead is everything"** - 80% du succès
2. **"Your first sentence must make them read the second"** - Momentum
3. **"Match the lead to the audience awareness"** - Stage approprié
4. **"Use bucket brigades to maintain flow"** - Transitions
5. **"Be ultra-specific"** - Chiffres précis

### 🛠️ APPLICATION PRATIQUE

**Framework de Création de Lead :**

**ÉTAPE 1 : Déterminer l'Audience Awareness**
- Unaware → Story ou News lead
- Problem Aware → Problem-Solution lead
- Solution Aware → Promise lead
- Product/Most Aware → Offer lead

**ÉTAPE 2 : Choisir le Type de Lead**
- Listez 3 options différentes
- Story vs Promise vs Problem

**ÉTAPE 3 : Écrire 3 Versions**
- Version A : Story lead
- Version B : Promise lead
- Version C : Problem-Solution lead

**ÉTAPE 4 : Ajouter Bucket Brigades**
- Insérez transitions toutes les 2-3 phrases

**ÉTAPE 5 : Vérifier le 4-Leg Stool**
- Urgency ?
- Usefulness ?
- Unique ?
- Ultra-specific ?

### 📊 EXEMPLE CONCRET

**Produit :** Programme copywriting

**Version A - Story Lead :**
"Three years ago, I was $50,000 in debt. My 'copywriting business' was making $0. I was about to quit and get a 'real job.'

Then, by accident, I discovered a simple formula that changed everything.

Within 6 months, I had my first $10,000 month. Within a year, I hit six figures. And I did it without any special talent, creativity, or 'gift for writing.'

What was the formula? I'm about to share it with you..."

**Version B - Promise Lead :**
"How to write sales copy that converts at 5%, 10%, even 20% – even if you're a complete beginner with zero writing experience...

In the next 7 minutes, I'm going to reveal the exact copywriting formula I've used to generate over $100 million in sales for my clients...

This isn't theory. This isn't 'creative writing.' This is a proven, step-by-step system that anyone can follow..."

**Version C - Problem-Solution Lead :**
"Are you tired of writing copy that falls flat?

Of sales pages that get traffic but no sales?
Of emails that get opened but don't get clicked?

You're not alone. According to recent industry data, 95% of marketing copy fails to convert.

But here's the thing: It's not your fault. You've been taught copywriting all wrong...

There's a simple formula that the top 1% of copywriters use. And once you know it, everything changes..."
